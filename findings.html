Discussion of Findings

By combining the heuristic evaluation and usability testing, we identified three major usability issues that affect the user experience of ChooZ.

1. Lack of Feedback After Saving a Decision

Related heuristic: 1 – Visibility of system status.

Several participants mentioned that after tapping the “Save” button, they were unsure whether the decision was logged. The system provides no visual confirmation (e.g., toast message or screen change). This violates the principle of keeping users informed about what is happening.

2. Random Pick Overlay Cannot Be Closed Easily

Related heuristic: 3 – User control and freedom.

The random pick overlay lacked a visible close (X) button. Users felt trapped and had to tap outside the overlay to close it – an action that was not obvious. This reduces user confidence and control.

3. Missing Legend for Color‑Coded Categories

Related heuristics: 2 – Match with real world, 6 – Recognition rather than recall.

While participants appreciated the color‑coded category chips (orange = Food, blue = Work, etc.), they did not understand what each color meant without trial and error. A simple legend on the Home screen would remove guesswork.
