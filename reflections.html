Group Reflections

Throughout the development and evaluation of ChooZ, each team member learned valuable lessons about HCI principles, usability testing, and collaborative design.

Gene Vincent Mañago (Leader)

Leading the group taught me how to balance design consistency with individual creativity. Creating the style guide and integrating all screens was challenging but rewarding. The usability testing revealed that even small issues (e.g., missing close buttons) drastically affect user trust. I learned that user feedback is not criticism – it is the quickest path to improvement.

Carol Ann Castillo

Designing the Add Decision screen taught me about form usability – too many fields scare users. After testing, we reduced required fields and added a confirmation. I now appreciate how small design tweaks can reduce cognitive load. Also, the onboarding screens should be skippable but informative. This project made me think like a user, not just a designer.

Julie Anne Egagamao

Working on History and Detail screens taught me about information architecture. Users need to find past decisions quickly – search and filters are essential. I learned that consistency in layout (same card design everywhere) makes the app feel professional. The back button is more important than I thought.

Exequiel Celedonio

Building the Categories and Statistics screens showed me how to visualize data without overwhelming users. Color coding helps, but a legend is necessary. I also learned that users love seeing patterns – the statistics page became one of the most appreciated features. Data without visualization is just numbers.

Michelle Abalos

The Settings and Random Generator taught me about user control. Toggles and options give users a sense of ownership. The random pick feature, while playful, must be usable – the close button issue was a big lesson. I also learned that even small animations (like a loading spinner) can improve perceived performance.

Overall takeaway: Usability is not a one‑time check – it requires iteration, real user testing, and willingness to change. The SUS scores and open‑ended comments guided our final recommendations. We are proud of ChooZ and excited to implement the suggested improvements.
